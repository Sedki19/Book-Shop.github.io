POSTWAR - A HISTORY OF EUROPE SINCE 1945

Tony Judt

Vintage Uk- 3 Juin 2010

À propos

FINALIST FOR THE PULITZER AWARD A magisterial and acclaimed history of post-war

New York Times Ten Best Books of the Year Europe in 1945 was drained. Much of the

continent was devastated by war, mass slaughter, bombing and chaos. Large areas of Eastern

Europe were falling under Soviet control, exchanging one despotism for another. Today, the

Soviet Union is no more and the democracies of the European Union reach as far as the borders

of Russia itself. Postwar tells the rich and complex story of how we got from there to here,

demystifying Europe's recent history and identity, of what the continent is and has been.
